sarima

SARIMA is a time series forecasting model that captures trends, seasonality, and autocorrelation in data. It combines autoregressive (AR), differencing (I), and moving average (MA) components, along with seasonal parameters, to model complex temporal patterns. It’s ideal for data with clear seasonality and trends but requires careful parameter tuning.

Regression

Linear Regression predicts a dependent variable (e.g., house prices) based on one or more independent variables (e.g., interest rates, income). It assumes a linear relationship between inputs and outputs, making it simple and interpretable. It’s widely used for its ability to incorporate external factors but struggles with nonlinear relationships.

Holt-winters

Holt-Winters is a time series forecasting method that models level, trend, and seasonality in data. It uses exponential smoothing to give more weight to recent observations, making it effective for short-term forecasts with seasonal patterns. It’s simple and efficient but limited to univariate data and less suitable for long-term predictions.
